 Reporting

A report is a presentation of information, typically taken from some form of data management system. Given the limited visual capabilities of early computer monitors and printers - and the computing power required to create complex visualisations - many reports were text-based. At the same time, the ability of reports to reshape information using things such as pivot tables, or to consolidate data from different systems and sources made them essential. Historically, different types of information system existed to produce different types of report - a transaction processing system would support day to day operations, whereas a decision support system would help guide more strategic decisions.

A dashboard is more complex than a report, though there is not a single distinguishing element. Some dashboards are described as such because such views use a number of different visualisation tools to present information. Other dashboards distinguish themselves through the provision of live or comparatively up-to-date information. Another distinguishing element might be interactivity, given the ability of more complex intelligence tools to transform views according to user input.
