Service Management at Scale

Alongside the Country Store example, we are going to explore an additional case study. CERN is the European Nuclear Research Organisation, the largest experimental physics laboratory in the world. An international collaboration between 23 member states and a range of associates and observers, the laboratory plays host to tens of thousands of researchers each year. While the Large Hadron Collider, the world's largest particle accelerator, frequently takes the headlines, the laboratory actually features a number of complex accelerators. The Web and Digital Technology

As the above illustrates, researchers at CERN are distributed across different labs, scattered across a large campus. Sharing information between different labs - and with colleagues at other institutions around the world - was a key concern for scientists. CERN played host to a range of different experiments in information exchange, including the W3 or 'World Wide Web' project, developed by Tim Berners Lee and collaborators. While internet technologies predate the web, the web allowed for a network of 'hyperlinked' pages. Digital technologies provided the means for operation of the complex research machinery, as well as the analysis of experimental data. As such, maintaining technology services is a major priority for the institution.
