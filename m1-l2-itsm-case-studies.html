The case for ITSM

In the fictional case study described in the course, the Country Store has grown from a rural shop into a global concern with 22 stores located around the world, and a growing online presence.  As the company has grown, it has embraced information technology, with departments adopting information systems according to their business area as well as spreadsheets and word documents. Complex and intricate integrations exist between the various systems and repositories that have developed over time. Given the increasing scale of the company, technology failures can be incredibly expensive. Management has decided to bring in a more structured approach to the management of IT services within the organisation.

As such, they've reached out to Cloud Dimensions, an imagined ServiceNow partner with a background in digital transformation. Cloud Dimensions neither has the resources nor the inclination to fix every single error in the systems of their client; they are not going to replace the entire technology infrastructure overnight. Instead, what they will attempt to do is configure a platform to allow the Country Store to gain visibility over the types of services they have, and who they are requested by. They will allow incidents to be reported in a centralised space, and recurring issues to be identified. They will keep track of changes that are to be made to IT Services, preventing a fix in one area from bringing down the rest of the system.
