The Importance of Data for Modern Organisations

“There are few corporate blunders as staggering as Kodak’s missed opportunities in digital photography, a technology that it invented.”

— Chunka Mui (2012)

The ability (or failure) to optimise data, has been likened to a Kodak Moment for big businesses. The crux of Kodak’s failure is widely acknowledged as their inability to recognise the game-changing, disruptive technology, which unseated its position as an industry leader. 

Innovations in data acquisition and modelling are evident across all sectors and, as the technology evolves, organisations need to respond rapidly to capitalise on this progress.

How Kodak failed

McKinsey’s Business Technology Office released findings that demonstrate that:

"The use of big data will become a key basis of competition and growth for individual firms. From the standpoint of competitiveness and the potential capture of value, all companies need to take big data seriously. In most industries, established competitors and new entrants alike will leverage data-driven strategies to innovate, compete and capture value from deep and up-to-real-time information” (Manyika et al., 2011).

They also acknowledged that these results were consistent across every sector they examined.
