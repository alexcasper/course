Request management in often works in tandem with Service Portal, but the two processes can be separated. For example, we can use the service portal only to generate records within tables, using record producers, skipping the system developed to handle specific requests. Similarly, it is possible to generate requests directly, for example from another enterprise product or even a communication tool.

What are the advantages of implementing a request management system alongside a service portal? And what are the challenges of developing them together?

As a stretch exercise, read Atlassian's introduction to how Jira Service Management handles request management. Atlassian is best known for its project tracking tool Jira and its knowledge management system Confluence, but its new product, Jira Service Management, has entered the service management space, just as ServiceNow has expanded to provide offerings in other areas.
