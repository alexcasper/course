Key Strategies behind successful digital transformations

As previously noted, US $1.3 trillion was spent on DT in 2019 alone, yet it is estimated that only 30% of this was deployed successfully, with US $900 billion wasted. MGI research found that only "16% of surveyed respondents say their organizations’ digital transformations have successfully improved performance and also equipped them to sustain changes in the long term. An additional 7 percent say that performance improved but that those improvements were not sustained."

These challenges were felt even in traditionally digitally savvy industries such as media, although company size did make a major difference. Smaller companies with employees less than 100 faired up to 2.7x better than companies with over 50,000 employees (McKinsey , 2018).

In the previous HBR article we learnt of the importance of purpose in galvanising organisations to successfully achieve digital transformation. In response to plunging natural gas prices in the wake of the global financial crisis that created hugely challenging conditions for Denmark's biggest energy company, Danish Oil and Gas, Henrik Poulsen, the company's new CEO hired from LEGO, stated: 

“We saw the need to build an entirely new company. It had to be a radical transformation; we needed to build a new core business and find new areas of sustainable growth. We looked at the shift to combat climate change, and we became one of the few companies to wholeheartedly make this profound decision, to be one of the first to go from black to green energy." 

This purpose driven strategic foundation was identified by HBR as a commonality across their top 20 transformations of the decade, but what are further critical factors that determine the likelihood of success?

  • MGI's research has found that with certain factors in place, surveyed respondents were up to 3X more likely to report successful transformations at their workplace.

    These factors include: 
    1. Digitally capable leaders in place
    2. Workforce capabilities exist aligned to the company's digital future
    3. People empowered to work in new ways
    4. Business tools upgraded to support new ways of working
    5. Communication happened frequently across traditional and digital channels
