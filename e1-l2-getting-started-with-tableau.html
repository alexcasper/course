Getting started with Tableau

 

In this section, we will focus on the basic aspects of data analysis, starting with the definition of different data types. As we discussed in previous sections, data usually come in two different formats, that's it the discrete (countable) or continuous (measurable) variables.

 Exploring the data types

Data may come from a population or from a sample. Most data can be put into the following categories:

  • Quantitative data are the result of counting or measuring the attributes of a dataset. This category of data is also called numerical data and may be either discrete or continuous.
  • Qualitative data are the result of categorizing or describing attributes of a dataset. This category of data is called also descriptive data, for example, high or low, bad or good.
  • Discrete data is information that can only take certain values. These are fixed values for example a child might have a shoe size of 3.5 but a child cannot have a shoe size of 3.72. 
  • Continuous data is data that can take any value. Height, weight, temperature and length are all examples of continuous data. Some continuous data will change over time; the weight of a person during the years or the temperature in a room throughout the day. This data is best shown on a line graph as this type of graph can show how the data changes over a given period of time.

In Tableau, we might need to work with all the aforementioned categories, but in reality, in most cases, we will have to deal with qualitative datasets. Now, let's move forward to the use of Tableau.
