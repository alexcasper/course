 Workflows

Service Now Flow Designer

Ultimately the strength of a platform is both in the information it provides and the tasks it facilitates. The Flow Designer is a low-code tool to effectively automate processes within an organisation. Many other information platforms have equivalents. Ultimately, the advantages of low-code development environments is that they make customising and expanding the potential of platforms more accessible. Rather than development being only accessible to a small cohort of experts, the low-code movement promotes the idea of the citizen developer - an individual in an organisation who is not a specialised developer. System administrators and business analysts, who often have a clearer understanding of requirements for a particular area, can often solve problems themselves.

Prior to the flow designer, another tool, called Workflows, served a similar function within ServiceNow.  Parts of ServiceNow still make use of these traditional workflows, and it is likely these will be preserved for some time to allow customers' specialised workflows to be maintained. In some situations, developers, analysts and implementation specialists will need or prefer to write code instead. There are a number of different types of scripting that are possible within the platform; these will be explored later.

Within the Flow Designer, there are triggers - these are the inciting incident that cause the flow to be run. There are actions, which involve manipulating data, inside of ServiceNow or outside of it. Meanwhile, Subflows allow functionality to be shared across multiple flows - this abstraction and reuse is critical to programming. Alongside the Flow Designer, the Process Automation Designer allows for an organisation to visualise and connect together flows and integrations across an organisation or business unit.
