Applying ITSM on ServiceNow

ServiceNow is a cloud workplace productivity platform used by many significant organisations. While it has expanded to provide solutions in a variety of areas, it has its origins as a cloud based tool for supporting service management in organisations. While commercial software and database solutions existed, ServiceNow's key innovation was that it hosted the solution on its own servers, thereby providing an early example of 'Software as a Service', a cloud delivery model. As well as providing a ready to go ('out of the box') solution for organisations, it also allowed developers to create their own tables, scripts, and applications using the platform; in that sense it was also an example of 'Platform as a Service'.

Effectively, the cycle described within IT Service Management bears similarities to the cycle developed by consultants such as W. Edwards Deming and Kaoru Ishikawa. These thinkers introduced into industry ideas of continuous improvement and monitoring of production processes.
