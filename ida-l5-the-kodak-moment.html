The Kodak Moment

The ability to analyse data is becoming increasingly important for organisations, with some likening it to a Kodak moment for big business. The crux of Kodak’s failure is widely acknowledged to be their inability to recognise a game-changing, disruptive technology, which unseated its position as an industry leader.

Kodak invented the first digital camera in 1975, but the company’s management during the 1980s and 1990s was concerned that digital products would cannibalise sales of their existing film products. While Kodak continued to pursue film products, their competitors (Canon and Sony) made massive gains in this part of the market.
