This section explores two techniques for sustaining IT Services within an organisation. Both techniques are described in the ITIL methodology, but are also present in other service management and support methodologies. 

Problem Management, which will be explored in the next module, has a different focus. Rather than simply resolving an issue, problem management attempts to identify patterns of service outage or failure, determining the cause and developing solutions or work arounds. Problems are frequently generated out of secondary analysis of incidents, but they are independent of them. When problem solving, we are not responsible to any particular user.

Incident Management, explored in this module, is focused simply on determining the nature of the problem to allow service to be restored as quickly as possible. Unlike problems, incidents will have a particular person responsible for raising the issue - the caller. Frequently, we can identify similarity between specific incidents. If an incident is already being addressed, two or more incidents can be linked as parent incident and child incident. This allows comments to be shared between incidents, often allowing for much quicker resolution of related issues. At the same time, it is important to note that with incidents, the focus is not on diagnosing the underlying problem, but rather on restoring service as quickly as possible.

Personas

Review the following personas:

End User An end user is responsible for raising a particular issue as the caller. They may be an external customer, or someone within the organisation in a non-IT support role.

Process Owner A process owner has oversight of a particular support service. For example, someone owning the incident process will have oversight over how this process is governed.

Service Owner A service owner will have oversight over a particular service. While not directly responsible for incident or problem resolution, they will follow this process to ensure quality service provision.

Incident Manager An incident manager will have oversight over the incident management process specifically, supporting service desk and technician personnel to resolve incidents.

Major Incident Manager A major incident manager will have oversight over incidents with a significant level of impact or potential impact. A more rapid and collaborative response is necessary, often spanning different teams or departments.

Service Desk Agent A service desk agent is responsible for directly communicating with end users to determine the nature of actual or potential incidents, providing updates or soliciting further information.

Level 2 Support Technician A level 2 technician is often called out to directly resolve incidents in the field. They will have a broad technical knowledge and be able to implement a range of possible fixes.

Level 3 Support Technician Level 3 technicians are typically specialists in one or more technology platforms. They may be more responsible for designing new fixes or working on complex, specialist problems beyond the scope of regular technicians.

Level 4 Support Technician Level 4 technicians are typically outside support agents, working for vendors or hardware manufacturers. Typically, they will not be brought in to solve most incidents, but may intervene in particularly serious or complex incidents.

Terminology

Review the following terms:

Incident An incident is an unplanned reduction in service quality.

Request A request is a communicated need for a service or resource.

Problem A problem is the underlying cause of one or more incidents, or the potential cause of future incidents.

Workaround A workaround is a temporary solution to identified problems that resolves incidents without resolving the underlying problem.

Root Cause The root cause is the identified ultimate cause of a service disruption or failure. Typically it is identified through root cause analysis.

Known Error A known error is an identified problem that does not have a permanent solution or workaround that can be implemented. It is registered, helping us to resolve and identify potential incidents.
