  Course Overview 

 

Course Description

This course explores how a wide range of enterprises around the world use information and information technology to create better managed, more innovative, and successful organisations. The 21st century enterprise runs on information, and every part of the business has been transformed by the use of information technology. Todayâ€™s business leaders, therefore, must have ready access to timely, accurate, and relevant information to manage effectively in the global economy. You will also have the opportunity to apply your knowledge of data management systems using industry standard cloud-based technology e.g. ServiceNow.

 

Assessments

 

Credit Assessments

You will have one ungraded (formative) and two graded (summative) assignments in this course:

The ungraded Formative assessment is due in module 3. Remember, the formative assignment does not contribute to your course mark but will help prepare you for your next two assignments. We ask that anyone intending to submit summative assessments submit a formative assessment. 

The first assignment is called Summative 1: Written assignment (workplace case study). It is recommended you complete the assignment incrementally as you complete each module. This assignment is due no later than Friday 23:59 Week 7.

The second assignment is called Summative 2: Computer-based examination. The exam will consist of multiple choice questions and will be 60 minutes in duration. The exam will be held on the Friday of Week 10 at 10:00.

Course Outcomes

KNOWLEDGE AND UNDERSTANDING

1 Understand the underlying principles and concepts of data management systems.
2 Develop information literacy and planning knowledge by comparing and contrasting various options for IT project and portfolio management and how each aligns with different approaches of digital governance.
3 Understand the role of emerging information technologies in producing both strategic opportunities and strategic threats to organizations.

SUBJECT SPECIFIC SKILLS

1 Classify various types of business information systems and how they meet organisational needs by addressing information problems across the value chain.
2 Apply analytic frameworks to assess the strategic value of information systems.
3 Apply descriptive frameworks to specify and mitigate the risks of ethical and legal issues involved with information assurance (information quality and cybersecurity).

TRANSFERABLE AND PROFESSIONAL SKILLS

1ai Demonstrate skill in numeracy and computation.
1aii Display a developing technical proficiency of written English skills that demonstrates an ability to communicate clearly and accurately when producing structured and coherent pieces of text.
2 Relate knowledge of theory to practice.
3 Engage in critical thinking.
