Summary & Looking Ahead

 

We started this module by looking into corporate governance, by which business corporations are directed and controlled. IT Governance is a sub-discipline and you were introduced to several IT Governance Frameworks like ISO 38500 and third-party standards.

Information technology is continually evolving based on the laws and rules of acceptable conduct. The ease in which information is communicated and copied in online environments poses new challenges related to privacy and intellectual property. The main ethical, social, and political issues information systems raise centre on information rights, property rights, accountability, and quality of life.

New information technologies and systems have an impact on private and business life.  These developments have also impacted legislation, and information systems have to be legally compliant in storing, processing, or transmitting information. We looked, therefore, into UK laws related to information systems and data protection.

In the next module, you will round up this course and start your practical experience with an information system by getting an introduction and hands-on experience on an industry-recognised platform – ServiceNow.

 

Learning Objectives

This week you have been learning to:

  • Develop information literacy and planning knowledge by comparing and contrasting various options for IT project and portfolio management and how each aligns with different approaches of digital governance
    • Outline the underlying concept of the IT Governance framework.
    • Recall different IT Governance frameworks.
    • Explain the six principles of good IT governance.
  • Understand the role of emerging information technologies in producing both strategic opportunities and strategic threats to organisations
    • Discuss the role of Information Assurance and its subdomains.
  • Apply descriptive frameworks to specify and mitigate the risks of ethical and legal issues involved with information assurance (information quality and cybersecurity)
    • Understand the role of UK laws in regards to information systems.
  • Research a topic independently to extract and synthesise information from a range of academic and online sources
  • Relate knowledge of theory to practice
  • Engage in critical thinking
