Module 6: Portals

Icon Progress Bar/Navigation (built in browser, hidden in app)

This week, we will investigate the centrality of service portals to business operations, both inside and outside of technology-centric organisations. We will look at how online commerce has changed our understanding of how we acquire goods and services, and how this has subsequently affected the way we work. While it originated in IT departments, the concept of service design has now become valuable for organisations across industries and the public sector. We will look at how ServiceNow facilitates the construction of a service catalogue, allowing information to be easily captured about requests.

 

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you should be able to:

  • Understand the role of emerging information technologies in producing both strategic opportunities and strategic threats to organisations
    • Discuss the value of having accessible information technology services to an organisation.
    • Understand the value of data analysis for process analysis and optimisation.
  • Classify various types of business information systems and how they meet organisational needs by addressing information problems across the value chain
    • Describe the value of service catalogs for supporting information capture and process optimisation.
  • Engage in critical thinking
    • Discuss the importance of low-code programming tools in business contexts.
