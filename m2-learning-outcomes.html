Summary & Looking Ahead

 

In this module, you started to explore the direct connection between information systems and business performance. We described the main business objectives driving the use of information systems and technologies in corporations all over the world: operational excellence, new products and services, customer and supplier intimacy, improved decision making, competitive advantage, and survival. We looked into the different dimensions of an information system, and you were introduced to the sociotechnical approach when studying information systems.

In the next module, we will look into business processes and how they are related to information systems. You will explore how systems serve different management groups in a business and will be introduced to the main types of Information systems.

 

Learning Objectives

This week you covered the following learning objectives:

  • Understand the role of emerging information technologies in producing both strategic opportunities and strategic threats to organisations
    • Discuss how information systems transform business and why they are essential for running and managing a business today.
    • Discuss the management, organisation and technology components of an information system.
  • Classify various types of business information systems and how they meet organisational needs by addressing information problems across the value chain
    • Describe the reasons why information systems are built and how they to contribute to corporate value.
  • Engage in critical thinking
    • Discuss why information systems are seen as sociotechnical systems.
