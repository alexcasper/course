Module 3: Tasks

Icon Progress Bar/Navigation (built in browser, hidden in app)

This week, we will learn about the idea of tracking and supporting tasks and activities. One of the key contributions of information systems is their ability to improve our efficiency, both in the workplace and outside of it. Many routine tasks can be automated, while more complex tasks can be facilitated through the provision of relevant, up-to-date information. Using information systems to manage tasks supports team members working remotely to collaborate, allowing teams to be distributed across time zones and continents. We will explore the importance of tasks in ServiceNow, and see how tasks can be distributed, worked and visualised.

 

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you should be able to:

  • Understand the role of emerging information technologies in producing both strategic opportunities and strategic threats to organisations
    • Discuss how information systems transform businesses and why they are essential for running and managing businesses today.
    • Discuss the management, organisation and technology components of an information system.
  • Classify various types of business information systems and how they meet organisational needs by addressing information problems across the value chain
    • Describe the reasons why information systems are built and how they to contribute to corporate value.
  • Engage in critical thinking
    • Discuss why information systems are seen as socio-technical systems.
