 Forms

While lists are used to view an entire dataset, or to compare records from a single dataset, a form is used to explore a particular record in detail. While we are trying to resolve a particular incident, assist a particular user, or determine the consequences of a particular change, we are interested in interacting with one single record. We might also want to see records associated with that particular record, called related lists in ServiceNow. Note that while we normally think of forms as ways of collecting information, in ServiceNow forms are used to view or modify existing information also.

The Form View

 

 

ServiceNow Forms

The goal of a form is to collect data in an organised way. Form Views in ServiceNow specifically are also used to review and modify existing data. Using a specific data type on the form will mean that the input method changes - while a text field will allow input, a date will have a calendar picker, a reference will allow a record from another table to be selected, and choice will present a number of different options. Forms can be customised to show or hide information depending on requirements, and certain fields can be made mandatory. Templates also make it easier to fill out forms more quickly.
