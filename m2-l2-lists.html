 Lists and Filters

The main way of viewing tables in ServiceNow is in the form of lists. Lists feature the row-as-record and column-as-field pattern. Tables in ServiceNow have often been designed to anticipate complex use cases, meaning that they have a large number of columns. As they store information about the operations of large organisations, they will also have a large number of records. This means we often will need to reduce the amount of information we directly have access through the use of filters and views. List views allow us to specify which columns are visible and accessible at a particular time. A filter can be used to specify which records are returned, based on the values in one or more fields.

Lists and Filters

Lists and Filters are hugely important for allowing us to review and compare multiple records at the same time. Effectively, we can think of lists and filters as the spreadsheet view within ServiceNow. Which columns are visible will depend not only on the application and its underlying tables, but also which view we have selected - given the amount of information stored within the system, a large number of columns are hidden by default. Ultimately, as power users we need to configure systems to the information we need regularly is accessible, clear and available, while also retaining the ability to access more specific information. As administrators, we want to set up our platforms in such a way that they our users can obtain the information they need easily.
