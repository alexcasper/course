Module 4: Overview


Information systems (IS) impact companies in many ways including economical, span of control and behaviours that are felt throughout the organisation. In this module, you will learn how IS continue to evolve and will look at competitive advantage including Porter's competitive forces model and the value chain model. You will also look at the challenges posed by IS, balancing this with business objectives and the challenges when transitioning and changing an information system.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you should be able to:

  • Understand the role of emerging information technologies in producing both strategic opportunities and strategic threats to organisations
    • Describe the impact of information systems on organisations.
  • Classify various types of business information systems and how they meet organisational needs by addressing information problems across the value chain
    • Discuss how Porter's competitive forces model, the value chain model, synergies, core competencies and network economics help companies develop competitive strategies using information systems.
  • Apply analytic frameworks to assess the strategic value of information systems
    • Explain how IT can be aligned with the business and use information systems.
    • Perform a strategic systems analysis.
  • Research a topic independently to extract and synthesise information from a range of academic and online source
  • Relate knowledge of theory to practice
  • Engage in critical thinking
