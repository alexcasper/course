 Course Overview and Theory of Relational Databases 

 

Databases play an important role in making our lives smoother every day. Watch this video to learn more.


Transcript

As you have seen, we are all, directly or indirectly, making use of databases in our everyday lives.

Databases play a huge role in business today – they can be said to be at the “core” of the business.

A database is a computer-based system, in the form of a logical structure that stores data (i.e. information). This data can include personal (and sensitive) information such as names, addresses, phone numbers, and medical histories; or items recently purchased from a supermarket or other online retailer.

However, it is not only a system for storing data, it permits the efficient searching and retrieval of information from the data stored within it. How efficient the searching and retrieval is will depend on how the database has been designed.

The database is in essence a collection of related data, and the software application that controls access to the data and manages it, is called a database management system (DBMS). 

 

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you should be able to:

  • Understand the basic principles of relational databases: their structure, use and how to interrogate them.
    • Explain what a database is in relation to its design and its use in the storage of different types of data.
    • Explain how to manage a relational database.
    • Identify the different environments of a relational database.
  • Have knowledge of the regulations, ethical and security considerations required in modern data storage.
    • Detail the ethical considerations concerning the processing of data within a relational database.
    • Be mindful of the security aspects required to keep information safe on a database.
