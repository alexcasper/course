Approvals in ServiceNow

Please complete the Approval section of IT Service Management Fundamentals. Digitising approval process within an organisation has the potential to save all departments - including IT itself - huge amounts of time and money, avoiding the unavoidable frustration that occurs when a request is missed, ignored, or not actioned in a timely manner.

Initially, it may be tempting for system administrators to review and approve requests themselves. They may indeed be asked to do so, particularly if people across the organisation are already overstretched, and are unfamiliar with the technologies used.

Why is it a bad idea for administrators to directly approve requests?

What can administrators and IT staff do to make sure the request management system is used?

Approving Requests

Complete the approve requests activity in the IT Service Management Fundamentals simulator. As with the previous activity, you start by impersonating someone - in this case this is the manager of the person who made the initial request. As the request totals more than $1000, the request requires approval, according to the default workflow used within the request management system. Who would be involved in setting up a new approval process within an organisation?

Complete the review status of a request activity. In this activity, you will review the status of a request that another user has made. A situation where this might be useful is if a user who is not familiar with the platform makes an inquiry at a helpdesk about the status of the item. Alternatively, a system administrator may wish to verify that the approval process is working, and that the correct approver has been assigned. Approval Flows

Within ServiceNow, there are two main methods of setting up approval flows - workflows and flows using flow designer. While flow designer is the newer option and is generally easier to use, a lot of organisations have already put a lot of resources into designing complex approval flows to match organisational requirements. There is no immediate pressure to convert all workflows into flows, and flows will continue to be supported. New approval processes should be designed with flow designer where possible.
