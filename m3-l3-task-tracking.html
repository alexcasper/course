 Task Tracking

The following suggests the data architecture of an organisation. The limitations of data architecture and information transfer mean there needs to be some autonomy granted to the individual groups. While an individual group responsible for business process might be able to sustain detailed knowledge of their own operations, they are unlikely to be able to maintain a detailed knowledge of any of the other groups' operations. They are therefore dependent on the central leadership for strategic guidance.

At the same time, the central leadership is limited by the fact that they are only ever able to access a summary or aggregate of the information. Their data is likely to be out of date. Therefore they need to delegate a lot of the day to day leadership to those within the groups, and focus on setting up rules or procedures that let groups know what is expected of them. Through the use of business rules,  management can effectively program how a group should respond to a certain situation. Through the use of operational level agreements, they can let different teams know what is expected of them. We can assure tasks are assigned to the relevant group using assignment rules, which determine which group or individual will have the requisite knowledge to deal with a problem. 

Groups benefit when they have up to date information to support their own operations, and access to up to date information about the rest of the organisation is doing. Individuals benefit when they can clearly see what they are responsible for, and how their work is affecting collective success.
