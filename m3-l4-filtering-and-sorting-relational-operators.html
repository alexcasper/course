Relational Operators

In many cases, there will be instances in which we are not looking for exact matches, and so we make use of Relational Operators and Logical Operators.

In short, relational operators compare two operands to see if they meet the specified information: TRUE if so; FALSE if not.

The relational operators in SQLite3 are ‘greater than’, ‘less than’, ‘greater than or equal to’, and ‘less than or equal to’.

Above or Below a Specified Value

When the relational operators are employed in SQL statements, records can be identified in which values are above or below a specified value. For example, if we were interested in attaining the customer invoices that exceeded $20 for customers by the customer ID number and Invoice ID number, we would use the following query.

SELECT CustomerID, InvoiceID, Total
FROM Invoice
WHERE Total > 20;

Range

We can also combine the relational operators to find records that fall into a particular range depending on a specific attribute, for example the customer invoices that lie in the range from 10 to 13 inclusive.

SELECT CustomerID, InvoiceID, Total
FROM Invoice
WHERE Total >= 10 AND Total <= 13;
