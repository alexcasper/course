Defining Data Management

The British Computer Society’s (BCS) Data Management Specialist Group has the following definition for Data Management:

"Data management is a corporate service which helps with the provision of information services by controlling or co-ordinating the definitions and usage of reliable and relevant data."

It is important to understand that most of the available data in a business does not belong to a dedicated data management (DM) unit. Most data is within the responsibility of different departments within the organisation. However, the data management function should provide the quality control over the organisation's data and be recognised as the authority concerning the enterprise's data.

As you will come to understand in the following modules, data management is the "little cousin" of information management (IM) as it supports the IM systems in their specific functions and provides the necessary infrastructure for data.

Data Management Responsibilities

Data management is a corporate service that strategically supports the corporate definition, management and use of business data. Furthermore, DM operationally supports the development and maintenance of computerised information systems.

To provide strategic and operational support, the DM function has several responsibilities:

  • Raising awareness that data is an organisation-wide valuable business resource
  • Improving data quality in the organisation's information systems and ensuring processes are in place to maintain data quality 
  • Providing corporate-wide data definitions to teams involved in the development of information systems
  • Making the various levels of management within the business accountable for the development and ownership of data definitions. This is part of the business and should not be handed over completely to the IT department
  • Achieving a single source for reference data to support all information systems within the enterprise, e.g. product codes and prices as well as country codes.

Information Service

When data management is implemented, data managers have a greater knowledge of what data and information is available within the organisation, where it is available and how it is used.  The Data Management function can provide a valuable information service to the business and can be a "one-stop-shop" for information about data and information.

 Deliverables:

  • Knowledgebase
  • Co-ordination of data usage

Education

Education and involvement of relevant stakeholders, at all levels, is key to the success of a Data Management initiative. This should happen at an early stage as the stakeholders need to understand the role they play in data management; from senior management through to end-users of information systems who may be responsible for collecting and inputting data, and technical staff in Information Technology (IT) or Information Security (IS) departments that need to follow and use the products delivered through data management.

Deliverables:

  • Training courses
  • Seminars
  • Articles

 

System Development Support

One factor for Data Management's success is based on how well it can influence the way data is defined and handled in new systems. Staff responsible for Data Management need to interact with developers of any future information system.

Deliverables:

  • Quality assured data models
  • Database schema

Data Management Tools

Data Management is a wide-ranging and complex activity and needs to be supported with automated tools that must be identified and procured. Furthermore, procedures need to be in place to ensure consistency and accessibility of data stored in these tools.

 Deliverable:

  • Managed set of repositories/ dictionaries populated to standards

Data Definitions

Developing corporate data definitions for the organisation is one of Data Management service's primary tasks. A data model / set of data models that contain the enterprise's information needs is developed. From these models derive the data definitions and each data definition must be owned by the appropriate business authority within the business (accountability).

 Deliverables:

  • Corporate data model
  • Identified data owners
  • Approved data definitions

Data Management Policy and Strategy

Another important, early task is the development of the organisation's Data Management policy and strategy.

The policy document should define the expectations from a Data Management initiative and describe the relationships between business managers, system end-users and IT staff related to the data management staff. This needs to be decided and authorised at the Senior Management level. Based on this policy, it is possible to develop a strategy of how to meet the data management goals and targets.

 Deliverables:

  • Policy documents
  • Plans
  • Road maps
