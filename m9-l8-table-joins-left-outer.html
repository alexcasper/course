The ORDER BY clause would be placed before the “LIMIT 20;” line, and would be written as:

ORDER BY C.CustomerID

The GROUP BY clause can also be used in conjunction with JOIN clauses.

It will group the rows with the same values into summary rows leading to the generation of summaries such as “total purchases of tracks or albums by each respective customer" .

If you recall, the GROUP BY clause tends to be employed when one of the aggregate functions are being used such as COUNT() or SUM(), and by that reckoning we would need to insert the SUM() function in our SQL statement as shown when you click on the tab below: 

Total Spend Per Customer

SELECT c.CustomerId, c.FirstName, c.LastName, SUM(i.Total)  Total

FROM Invoice i

LEFT JOIN Customer c

ON i.CustomerId = c.CustomerId

GROUP BY c.CustomerId

LIMIT 30;
