Data Management Systems

 

Welcome

This course explores how a wide range of enterprises around the world use information and information technology to create better managed, more innovative, and successful organisations.

  Course Introduction

Module 1: Data and the Enterprise

Welcome to Module 1!

This module explores the distinctions between data, information, knowledge and wisdom and the processes involved in the data transformation. You will get introduced to the different data quality dimensions and you will able to discuss the impact (e.g. costs) of poorly organised data in an organisation.

We will focus on the following learning objectives:

  • We will explore the underlying principles and concepts of data management systems
  • We will develop information literacy and planning knowledge by comparing and contrasting various options for IT project and portfolio management and how each aligns with different approaches of digital governance
  • We will explore the role of emerging information technologies in producing both strategic opportunities and strategic threats to organisations

Module 2: Information Systems Today

Welcome to Module 2!

This module explores the connection between information systems and business performance. We will describe the main business objectives driving the use of information systems and technologies in corporations all over the world: operational excellence, new products and services, customer and supplier intimacy, improved decision making, competitive advantage, and survival.

We will focus on the following learning objectives:

  • We will explore the role of emerging information technologies in producing both strategic opportunities and strategic threats to organisations
  • We will classify various types of business information systems and how they meet organisational needs by addressing information problems across the value chain
  • We will discuss and argue why information systems are seen as sociotechnical systems.

Module 3: Different Types of Information Management Systems 

Welcome to Module 3!

This module begins by focusing on how business activities and tasks are grouped to become business processes and goes through how business processes are mapped. It then discusses the four types of information systems found within the various levels across an organisation. Understanding these systems and how they relate to an organisation's business processes is vital to those within the information technology function.

We will focus on the following learning objectives:

  • We will explore how the main business objectives driving the use of information systems and technologies.
  • We will classify organisations into the four types of information systems
  • We will discuss and argue how these systems relate to an organisation's business processes

Module 5: Governance, Legal and Ethical Issues

Welcome to Module 5!

This module looks into corporate governance, which provides a framework of processes, practices and rules by which business corporations are directed and controlled. It also discusses ethical, social and political issues that information systems raise regarding information rights, property rights and accountability and laws that help protect individuals’ and companies’ data ownership and privacy rights.

We will focus on the following learning objectives:

  • Develop information literacy and planning knowledge by comparing and contrasting various options for IT project and portfolio management and how each aligns with different approaches of digital governance
  • Explore the role of emerging information technologies in producing both strategic opportunities and strategic threats to organisations
  • Explore descriptive frameworks to specify and mitigate the risks of ethical and legal issues involved with information assurance (information quality and cybersecurity)
