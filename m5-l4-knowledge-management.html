 Knowledge Management

A knowledge management system is a type of information repository that allows users to create knowledge bases; collections of knowledge articles that explain processes or technologies in use within an organisation. The concept of knowledge management as applied on modern information platforms owes a great deal to Wikipedia, an online encyclopaedia that is expanded and curated by its users, and to Stack Overflow, a community where individuals share coding best practices. The distinction between knowledge and information is typically that while information still needs to be interpreted, knowledge has already been applied to a particular domain or given enough structure so as to be useful.

Service Now Knowledge Management

In ServiceNow, Knowledge Management is connected closely to a number of areas. In IT Service Management, knowledge articles can be created from incidents, capturing the steps that were taken to resolve the incident. Similarly, knowledge articles can be generated from problems; this is particularly useful if an underlying issue has not been resolved, but there is a workaround or the risk has been accepted. Knowledge articles will then be visible to support staff as they attempt to resolve future issues. Common issues or problems can be captured and published; it maybe that users can then resolve some problems without having to seek help. Other parts of an organisation can also make use of knowledge management to disseminate information; for example, a human resources department will often share information about policies and benefits.
