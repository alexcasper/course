 Hands-On Practical Exercise (HOPE): Session 2

 

Type: Challenge-Based Learning

Description:

In addition to the current US-based client, your supervisor has assigned you to (i.e., “Just Lee Books”), you have also been temporarily assigned to assist in a small project for a colleague who is currently on annual leave for the week.

It is an in-house project involving the “Chinook multimedia database”. You will be expected by your supervisor to divide your time between the two projects as both have milestones to achieve by the end of the week!

Please allow 30 to 60 minutes to complete both stages before you move on to the solutions below.

You will not need to upload your attempts. However, you can choose to save your SQL queries in a separate text file or as screenshots if you want to keep a record of your attempts.

Stage 1: Chinook

You can use SQLite3 for this stage.

You should set .mode column and .header on for the output tables to display properly aligned columns and header names respectively.

You have been tasked by your supervisor to answer the following:

  • S1-1. What are the full names and city locations of the customers with customer ID values of 23, 33 and 42?
  • S1-2. Locate the employee who lives at an address that starts with ‘7727B’.
    • What is their role at the company?
  • S1-3. How many customer records are missing a postal code?
  • S1-4. What was the total value of orders placed in March 2009?

 

Stage 2: Just Lee Books

You can use SQLite3 for this stage.

  • For upload to SQLite3, download the database file from: JLDB_HOPE_1.db  

You should set .mode column and .header on for the output tables to display properly aligned columns and header names respectively.

In order to meet the requirements of the client, you may need to filter and sort the data, and/or perform joins and set operations, where appropriate.

The requests from the client are thus:

  • S2-1. List all order numbers and their ship dates for orders shipped after April 2, 2009.
  • S2-2. There is going to be a contest at the bookstores located in Florida and California. List all of the customers by first and last name that live in both states.
    • The results also need to be sorted in ascending order by last name within the state: California first followed by Florida.
  • S2-3. List the titles and cost for all books that contain the word “cook” in the title.
    • Sort by cost.
  • S2-4. List the book titles, their cost, retail and profit margin for all books that have a profit margin under 35%.
    • The profit margin is calculated as:

100 * [retail – cost]/retail

  • S2-5. List the book title, publisher name, contact, and contact phone number for all computer books with a retail price under $70.
  • S2-6. List all book titles and the first and last names of the customer(s) who ordered each title.
    • Be sure to show the titles of the books that were not ordered.
  • S2-7. List the publishers who have not published any cooking books.
